# GatePass Doctrine

## Controlling statement

> GatePass is the association-owned operating system that routes property work from an exterior signal to permission, verified execution, and a permanent record. Contractors pay for trusted access to communities; associations retain control of the workflow and data.

This statement controls product scope, public copy, onboarding, pricing, demo behavior, investor framing, internal implementation choices, and future feature decisions.

## Operative sequence

```text
exterior signal
→ association permission
→ trusted contractor access
→ verified execution
→ permanent association record
```

### Exterior signal

A lawfully visible exterior condition or a homeowner- or association-supplied observation enters as a signal. It is not a diagnosis, inspection result, authorization to enter property, or authorization to perform work.

### Association permission

The association applies its governing documents, policies, and approval process. The association is the principal. A management company or individual property manager may operate inside GatePass without owning the workflow or data.

### Trusted contractor access

Contractors apply before payment. Approved contractors may enter association-controlled channels. Contractor payment does not buy association approval, ranking, a lead, project assignment, or guaranteed job volume.

### Verified execution

Verification means the defined records attached to the work: identity, credentials, applicable licenses, insurance, scope, association approvals, status, and completion evidence. It is not an endorsement, warranty, or quality guarantee.

### Permanent association record

The association owns the workflow history and can export it. Records do not disappear when a board member, vendor, property manager, or management company changes.

## Fixed boundaries

- The homeowner keeps private-property choice and contractor selection.
- GatePass does not diagnose property conditions from exterior observations.
- GatePass does not speak for an association before association permission exists.
- GatePass works alongside management companies; public copy does not promise full management-company replacement.
- AI may assist specific workflows. It is a feature, not the product category, operating method, or customer promise.
- GatePass is pre-revenue. Public product activity is modeled unless explicitly identified as production data.
- HOA software is displayed at $20 per unit per year with no setup fee.
- Founding contractor access is displayed at $99 once, after approval.
- Any contractor-funded association or homeowner economic benefit remains counsel-gated and is not a current public promise.
- Product doctrine changes require contact from reality: a board conversation, contractor response, investor response, counsel ruling, or deployment result.

## Decision test

A feature, sentence, or workflow belongs in GatePass only when it preserves all five conditions:

1. A property-work signal enters without being misrepresented as final truth.
2. The association retains the permission decision.
3. Contractor access remains approved, bounded, and non-guaranteed.
4. Execution produces attributable evidence.
5. The association owns the resulting record and export.

Anything that breaks one of these conditions is outside the doctrine until contact from reality changes the rule.
